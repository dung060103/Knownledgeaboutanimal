BRANCHIOSAURUS

Brachiosaurus is a genus of late Jurassic sauropod dinosaur from the Morrison Formation of North America. It was first described by Elmer S. Riggs in 1903 from fossils found in the Grand Canyon River (now Colorado River) of western Colorado, United States.

DIPLODOCUS

Diplodocus is one of the most recognizable dinosaurs, with its typical dinosaur-like appearance, long neck and tail, and sturdy four legs. For many years, it was still considered the longest known dinosaur.

Since its discovery, Spinosaurus has been considered one of the longest and largest Theropod genera. Both Friedrich von Huene in 1926 and Donald F. Glut in 1982 listed it as the largest Theropod species in their studies, with a length of 15 meters (49 ft) and a weight of 6t (5.9 long tons; 6 t; 6 ft). ,6 US tons).

STEGOSAURUS

Stegosaurus, (genus Stegosaurus), one of the various plated dinosaurs (Stegosaurs) of the Late Jurassic (159 million to 144 million years ago) recognizable by its pointed tail and a series of large bony plates triangle along the back. Stegosaurus typically grew to about 6.5 meters (21 feet) in length, but some species reached 9 meters (30 feet).

T-REX

Tyrannosaurus rex was a bipedal carnivore, balanced by a long, heavy tail. Compared to its large and strong hind limbs, the forelimbs of Tyrannosaurus were short but exceptionally strong for its size and had two clawed toes. Tyrannosaurus rex remains the largest standard tyrannosaur and one of the largest carnivores of all time.

TRICERATOPS

Having large bony frills and three horns on a large four-legged body, similar to the modern rhinoceros, Triceratops is one of the best known dinosaurs and the best known Ceratopsidae. It shared the environment and could have preyed on tyrannosaurs.
